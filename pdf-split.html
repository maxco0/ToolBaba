What Does "Splitting a PDF" Actually Mean?

Splitting a PDF is the process of creating a new document that contains a subset of pages from an original source file. It does not destroy the original file; it simply copies the selected pages and packages them into a fresh container.

There are generally two ways to split:

  • Extract Pages: You pick specific pages (e.g., Page 5, 6, and 7) and save them as a new, standalone file.
  • Burst Split: You take a 10-page document and "burst" it into 10 separate files, each containing one page.

Our tool focuses on the "Extract" method, giving you precise control over exactly what content makes it into your final document.
